A nonlinear [[Unsupervised Machine Learning#Dimensionality Reduction|dimensionality reduction method]]

Designed to address both [[Multi-Dimensional Scaling#Downsides|Downsides of MDS]]

SNE preserves the nearest **neighbors** in lower dimensional space, not *distances* (MDS)
- solves computational complexity problem
- solves problem of not being able to preserve distances in lower dimensional space

![[t-SNE example.png|500]]


## Method

**Loss Function:** [[Kullback-Leibler Divergence|KL Divergence]]

$$ L = \sum_{i, j}p_{ij}\log\frac{p_{ij}}{q_{ij}}$$
$p_{ij}$: High dimensional **affinity** (i.e. similarity between datapoints; kinda like distance)
- literally the "distance" between $x_i$ and $x_j$ in its high dimension
$$p_{j|i} = \frac{ \exp\left(-\frac{\|x_i - x_j\|^2}{2\sigma_i^2}\right) }{ \sum_{k \ne i} \exp\left(-\frac{\|x_i - x_k\|^2}{2\sigma_i^2}\right) }$$

$q_{ij}$: Low dimensional affinity
- "distance" between $y_i$ and $y_j$ in low dimension, or **image** of x

$$q_{ij} = \frac{\exp\left(-\|y_i - y_j\|^2\right)}{\sum_{k \ne i} \exp\left(-\|y_i - y_k\|^2\right)}$$
> [!warning] This is for Gaussian Kernel form, or SNE
> With t-SNE the $q_{ij}$ formula changes cuz its [[T-Distribution]]

## Learning and Perplexity

SNE minimizes loss by iteratively moving the points in low dimensional embedding

But, SNE is unlikely to preserve global structure of data (especially if initialized with a random low-dimensional configuration)
--> recommend initialize starting positions with [[Principal Component Analysis (ML)|PCA]]

**Perplexity**: hyperparameter of SNE, or the effective width of the neighborhood
- Default = 30
- Also determines kinds of cluster generated by SNE
	- Above 30: gaps between clusters tends to get larger
	- Below 30: gaps tends to get narrower

## SNE to t-SNE

**Crowding Problem**
basically, in high dimensional space, there really are no neighbors (everything's far away)
![[SNE crowding problem.png]]

We use [[T-Distribution]] instead of [[Normal Distribution]]
1. t-distribution approaches normal distribution with high degree of freedom
2. when small, t-distribution has *heavier tails* (extreme values more likely)

## T-SNE assessment

- Faster than [[Multi-Dimensional Scaling|MDS]]
- Often yields cleaner category separations than [[Principal Component Analysis (ML)|PCA]], especially when visualized (and t-SNE with high perplexity)
- Preserves local structure well (but not global structure)
- Still a bit slow $O(n^2)$
