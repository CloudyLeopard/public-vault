**What is Machine Learning?**

> "The field of study that gives computers the ability to learn without being explicitly programmed"
> - Arthur Samuel (1959)

> "Using computers to find patterns in data"
> - Daniel Bourke (2020)
> maybe you don't see the data, but the computer does

> "A model is said to learn from data if its performance on a given task improves after the data is taken into account"
> - Tom Mitchell (1997)

## Major Branches of Machine Learning
**Supervised** learning: Dataset consists of **labeled** data (input/output pairs) that are characterized by **feature** vectors
- Example: Classification
- Historically the oldest branch of machine learning. Very straightforward
**Unsupervised** learning: Dataset consists of **unlabeled** examples that are also characterized by feature vectors
- Example: Clustering
**Reinforcement** learning: The machine "lives" in a virtual **environment** (the **state** of which is encoded as a feature vector). It can execute **actions** and receive **rewards** to learn a **policy**
- Example: Driving
- Related to "agents"
