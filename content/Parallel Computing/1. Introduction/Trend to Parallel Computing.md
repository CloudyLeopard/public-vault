Prior to 2002, [[Moore's Law]] allowed computer scientists focus on reducing transistors to increase performance. This is supported because of [[Dennard Scaling]].

However, Dennard Scaling stopped in 2002! Power became a problem.

> [!important] Why did Power Become a Problem?
Power is important - power needed per chip can be divided into two parts:
> 1. **power consumed**: for processor to do the calculates needed to execute your programs
> 2. **power dissipated** (wasted): in the form of temperature
> 
> More power means:
> - exponential increase in cost of packaging
> - high electricity bill for big machines
> - shorter battery life for portable machines
>
> Power per chip increases due to two factors
> - increasing number of transistors per chip (after dennard scaling stopped)
> - increasing the frequency (i.e. clock scaling)
> 
> There's also other stuff, such as the heat generated cannot be cooled off easily, and requires liquid cooling or other expensive strategies.
