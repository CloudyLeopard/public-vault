**Dennard Scaling**: as we scale down transistors (i.e. transistors getting smaller), their *power density stays constant so that the power use stays in proportion with area*
- Reduce circuit delay (faster machine): as transistors shrink, the distance electrons need to travel decreases --> circuits and switch faster --> more instructions per second
- Increase operating frequency: CPU clock speed (in GHz) can be increased
- Reduced operating voltage $\rightarrow$ reducing power (while keeping power the same)
	- As transistors shrink, the operating voltage can also be reduced.
	- Lower voltage means less power is consumed per transistor.
	- Even though the number of transistors increases, the total power consumption remains nearly constant.


Dennard scaling is the reason why [[Moore's Law]] was so good.

**However, this is the case until 2002**. Power became a problem! Physical and electrical limitations broke the pattern seen with Moore's law and performance increase.
