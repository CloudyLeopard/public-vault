The number of transistors on microchips doubles every two years.

Moore's made this **prediction** with just five data points. It became a strategic decision made by firms - companies who move slower than Moore's law often became commodities.
- Interestingly enough, companies who try to move faster than Moore's law ends up *bankrupt*! Moore's law turns out to be exactly the right rate to plan for future chips

**Moore's did not mention anything about performance**, only the number of transistors. The best use of this change *depends on programmers*

Effect of Moore's law:
- ~1982 - 2002: 50% performance increase
- Since 2002: ~20% performance increase *only*.

This change in Moores law is because 2002 marks the **end of [[Dennard Scaling]]**.
