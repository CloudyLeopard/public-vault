**Core = CPU**: these can be used interchangeably, the piece of the hardware that *executes* the program.
- **Performance Core**: more execution units, so its faster, but it consumes more power.
- **Efficiency Core**: may not has as much execution units, have lower frequency but consumes less power.

Typically has their own private L1 [[Cache]]

> [!important] Distinction with Processor
> A [[Processor]] is *not* a core. A processor *contains* cores.

When a core becomes very warm/hot, the core will slow down using **dynamic voltage** and **frequency scaling** to avoid burning.
