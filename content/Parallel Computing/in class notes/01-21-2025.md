Moore's Law: number of transistors on microchips doubles every two years
- Note: he made this **prediction** with just five data points, and became very famous.
- It became a strategic decision made by firms. Companies who moves slower than moore's law often just becomes commodity
	- but, interestingly enough, companies who try to move faster than Moore's law in the end goes bankrupt. It turns out to be exactly the right rate for planning for your future chip.
- Also, he did not mention anything about performance. He only mentioned about number of transistors. Making the best use of it depends on *programmers*.

**Why did power become a problem?**
Power is important - power needed per chip can be divided into two parts:
1. **power consumed**: for processor to do the calculates needed to execute your programs
2. **power dissipated** (wasted): in the form of temperature
More power means:
- exponential increase in cost of packaging
- high electricity bill for big machines
- shorter battery life for portable machines
Power per chip increases due to two factors
- increasing number of transistors per chip (after dennard scaling soopped)
- increasing the frequency (i.e. clock scaling)

trend now: more core in chip and every core is slower (i think?)
since the trend is more core and weaker core, in order to make use of this type of chip (which is every where now), is to make *parallel code*.

**Multicore Processors Save Power**
$Power = C * V^2 * F$, $Performance = Cores * F$
($C$ = capacitance, $V$ = voltage, $F$ = frequency)
