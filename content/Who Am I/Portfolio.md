### Senior Honors Thesis: KRUPPE

In my senior year at NYU Stern, I was a part of NYU's Senior [Honors program](https://www.stern.nyu.edu/current-students/undergraduate/academics/research-opportunities/honors-program-theses). The program had 7 students. Each of us spent the entire academic year working on a research paper, with the guidance of an advisor.

I wrote my paper on a ==Large Language Model framework to answer financial queries== (questions that are more complex than simple fact retrieval). I propose **KRUPPE**, an Agentic LLM framework based off an [analyst decision making model proposed 30+ years ago](https://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/0361368287900134).

FYI, the name "KRUPPE" is short for "Knowledge-Based Research Using Pathway and Procedural Engine", and it means absolutely *nothing*. I just wanted to name my paper after a character from my favorite [[Favorite Books#Malazan Book of the Fallen|fantasy series]].
